# Fleshwarp
**Creature Type:** Other  
**Rarity:** Rare  
**Alignment:** Neutral  
**Source:** Homebrew

---
## Overview
Fleshwarps are not a species in the traditional sense, but the result of deliberate or accidental transformation. They are living bodies reshaped by arcane, alchemical, or experimental forces—be it through reckless ambition, controlled research, or catastrophic failure.
In places where magic is studied without restraint, Fleshwarps are an inevitability. Some are the byproduct of failed experiments, others are the intended result of attempts to refine or “perfect” life itself. While many assume them to be mindless or broken, this is not always the case. Some Fleshwarps retain full awareness, memory, and purpose—though often at a cost not immediately visible.
They occupy an uncertain place in the world: too unstable to be trusted, too valuable to be ignored.

---
## Appearance
No two Fleshwarps are truly alike.
At a glance, many still resemble their original form—humanoid silhouettes stretched just far enough to feel wrong. Limbs may be slightly too long, joints bending with unfamiliar articulation, or skin bearing faint seams, discolorations, or arcane scarring that never quite fades. Others show more pronounced alterations: asymmetry in posture, irregular breathing patterns, or subtle movements that don’t match conscious intent.
In more extreme cases, the body reflects the nature of what changed it. Bone may press too close to the surface, musculature shifting beneath the skin as if unsettled, or features settling into shapes that serve a function no natural body was meant to perform.
What is most consistent is not the form—but the sense that something is still adjusting.

---
## Behavior & Ecology
Fleshwarps do not share a unified ecology, as their existence is rarely natural.
Many are found near the places that created them—abandoned laboratories, sealed facilities, collapsed tunnels, or hidden research sites. Some remain where they were made, either by choice or circumstance. Others leave, attempting to integrate into society or disappear from it entirely.
Their behavior varies widely. Some cling to routine, repeating habits from before their transformation as if anchoring themselves. Others become intensely focused, driven by a singular purpose—often tied to the experiment that created them. A few exhibit instability, not always violent, but unpredictable, as if their body and mind are no longer fully aligned.
Fleshwarps do not reproduce in any conventional sense. New Fleshwarps arise only when the same conditions that created them are repeated—intentionally or otherwise.

## Lore & Hooks
- It is said that in certain hidden laboratories, Fleshwarps are not considered failures—but iterations. Each one logged, studied, and built upon.
- Some scholars believe Fleshwarps represent a transitional state, not a mistake—proof that life can be reshaped into something greater.
- Rumors persist of individuals who willingly undergo the transformation, seeking to become something beyond mortal limitation.
- Certain factions quietly collect Fleshwarps, offering protection in exchange for cooperation… or compliance.
- In Eldermount, whispers suggest that disturbances in the Lower Tunnels have revealed spaces that were never meant to be found—places where Fleshwarps were not just created, but designed.
