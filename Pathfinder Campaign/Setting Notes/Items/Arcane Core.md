## Description
The Arcane Core is a compact but intricate device of ancient dwarven make, formed from interlocking rings of runed black bronze rotating in silent gyroscopic motion. At its center floats a multifaceted crystal lattice of pure arcane essence, its innermost crystal densely engraved with layered sigils and formulae. The bronze plates shift in slow, deliberate alignment, refracting light into sharp prismatic fractures while faint glyphs shimmer across the metal’s surface. A low, resonant hum emanates from the structure, more felt in the bones than heard, and the air around it seems subtly distorted, as though reality is being pressed inward toward the crystal heart.

In operation, the Arcane Core functions as a self-sustaining arcane engine. It does not consume fuel, nor does it diminish with use. Instead, it continuously produces and stabilizes magical energy, reinforcing nearby enchantments and sustaining complex arcane systems indefinitely. Constructs powered by it operate without degradation, ritual arrays remain perfectly balanced, and spells anchored to it resist natural expiration. Yet its presence is not passive—ambient magic grows denser, planar boundaries thin, and prolonged exposure causes subtle environmental shifts, suggesting that the Core does more than generate power; it exerts influence.

Recovered notes from **Project Pheonix** refer to the device as a “Contained Arcane Star” and emphasize that it was never intended to be a mere generator. It was designed as the foundation for rebirth—an engine capable of sustaining life, consciousness, or essence beyond natural limits. Marginal annotations in later documentation grow increasingly terse, referencing “arcane oversaturation,” “autonomous recalibration,” and a final, underlined warning: _The Core is not empty. It is learning._

## Crafting
- **Requirements:** To recreate or replicate an Arcane Core, all of the following would be required:
	- **Ancient Black Bronze Alloy**  
	    A dwarven metallurgical formula predating modern guild records. The alloy must be forged in a furnace fueled by elemental flame or a bound primordial heat source.
	- **Condensed Arcane Essence Crystal**  
	    Not mined, cut, or shaped — but grown. This requires the controlled compression of raw arcane energy at a ley-line convergence or planar thin point over the course of months or years.
	- **Runic Containment Geometry**  
	    Master-tier Crafting and Arcana (Legendary proficiency recommended), capable of layering structural engineering with arcane mathematics.
	- **Planar Stabilization Framework**  
	    A permanent anchoring structure preventing the crystal from destabilizing into a planar breach.
	- **Living Essence Catalyst**  
	    Project Pheonix notes imply that a spark of living soul-energy was required to “ignite” the lattice. The method is redacted in surviving documentation.
- **Crafting DC:**  50, Legendary Proficiency in Arcana and Crafting, Failure by 10 or more results and catastrophic arcane destabilization
- **Formula:**  It would need to be scrounged and researched from Project Pheonix notes

This is the earliest functional version of the core, Its unstable and inefficient, the chamber made mostly out of normal bronze with enough [[Black Bronze]] to make it functional
#### Function
- Provides a weak but sustained magical power, but only to a singular attached construct or ritual array
- Magical effects powered by it cannot expire naturally but the device must be periodically re-calibrated

- Ay the end of each day it is used, roll a flat check (DC 10 + number of days used)
- On a failure, the device becomes [[Arcane Core#Degraded|Degraded]]
	- Roll a 1D6 and consult the following table
		- **1-3 - Arcane Discharge**
			- All creatures within 10ft must make a Reflex Save (DC 15)
				- **Critical Success:** No damage.
				- **Success:** Half damage.
				- **Failure:** 2d8 Electricity and 2d8 Fire damage.
				- **Critical Failure:** Max damage (16 Electric and 16 Fire) and Stunned 1 as arcane force courses through their body.
		- **4 — Construct Glitch**  
			- All Animated Constructs within 30 feet suffer a destabilizing arcane error.
				- They take a –1d6 status penalty to all attack rolls, skill checks, and saving throws.
				- This condition lasts until they spend 10 minutes recalibrating or are magically repaired.
		- **5 — Arcane Feedback Pulse**  
			- The Core emits a destabilizing harmonic wave.
				- All ongoing magical effects within 30 feet must attempt a counteract check against a DC 15
					- On a failure, one random spell effect ends prematurely.
				- Spellcasters in the area must succeed at a Fortitude Save (DC 15) or become Clumsy 1 and take a –1  penalty to spell attack rolls and spell DCs for 1 minute.
					- This effect does not discriminate between allies and enemies.
		- **6 — Planar Thinning Event**  
			- The barrier between planes weakens briefly around the Core.
				- For 1 minute, the area within 20 feet is treated as a **minor planar thin point**.
				- Illusory effects gain a -5 to their DC.
				- Teleportation and summoning effects require no material components.
				- At the end of the minute, roll a flat DC 10 check:
					- On a failure, a minor extraplanar entity is drawn to the disturbance (GM discretion).
### Degraded
When the Arcane Core becomes **Degraded**, its containment matrix is compromised. The bronze rings no longer rotate in perfect harmonic alignment, and visible fractures of arcane light bleed from the central crystal. The harmonic hum becomes irregular, pulsing in uneven intervals like an arrhythmic heartbeat.
