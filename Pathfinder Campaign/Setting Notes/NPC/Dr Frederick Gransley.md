## Personality
- **Demeanor:** Calm, clinical, and quietly assured. Gransley speaks with the patience of someone used to being correct, rarely raising his voice or showing urgency, even in situations that demand it.
- **Motivations:** To perfect the work of [[Project Pheonix]]. Gransley does not view it as dangerous or unethical—only incomplete. He believes transformation is not failure, but progression.
- **Flaws / Secrets:**
    - He has undergone multiple procedures himself, though to what extent is unclear.
    - He no longer distinguishes between subject and researcher in the way others would.
    - There are moments—brief, almost imperceptible—where his control slips, and something beneath surfaces.
- **Voice / Quirk:** Speaks softly, precisely, often choosing words with surgical care. Occasionally pauses mid-sentence, as if recalibrating his thoughts—or suppressing something else. Rarely blinks during conversation.

---
## Background
Dr. Frederick Gransley was once a respected figure within Eldermount’s academic circles, known for his work in alchemical theory and biological reconstruction. His early research focused on restoration—repairing damaged tissue, extending vitality, and stabilising magical influence within the body.
At some point, his work diverged.
Whether by invitation or necessity, Gransley became deeply involved in [[Project Pheonix]], contributing to its more experimental branches. Records from this period are incomplete, fragmented, or deliberately obscured. What remains suggests rapid advancement… followed by silence.
He was last known to be operating somewhere beneath the city, within or beyond the boundaries of the Lower Tunnels.

## Plot Role

- Gransley serves as a **central figure tied to the deeper truth of Project Pheonix**—not its creator, but its most refined result.
    
- He may be encountered as:
    
    - A composed researcher continuing his work in secret
        
    - A guide through deeper sections of the ruins, offering knowledge at a cost
        
    - Or something the party realises they should not have trusted
        

**Hooks / Scenes:**

- A laboratory still in operation, untouched by time, where Gransley continues his work as if nothing has changed
    
- Notes or recordings that speak of “stability” and “progress,” growing more abstract over time
    
- An encounter where Gransley appears entirely reasonable… until the party notices inconsistencies
    
- The reveal that some of the [[Infected]] are not failures—but steps toward what he has become
