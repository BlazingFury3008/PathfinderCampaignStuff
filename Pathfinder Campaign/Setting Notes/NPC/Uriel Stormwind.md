## Appearance
Uriel Storming is unmistakenly draconic at a glace, less a man with dragons blood and more a dragon forced into a mortal shape. Broad and imposing, he stands heavy, grounded posture of something build for strength rather than speed, his presence filling the space behind the bar of the [[Dragonclaw Tavern]] as surely as the fireplace's heat fills the room.
His body is covered in dense, interlocking silver scales, dull in shadow but catching the firelight in sharp metalic flashes. The scales are thicker along his shoulders, forearms and spine, forming a natural protective armour that gives him a more sculpted silhouette, whereas the more flexible scales lining his neck and joints subltly shift as he moves.
His head is fully draconic in shape, a pronounced snout and rigid brow, with a crown of sweeping horns that frame his skull. Despite all this, there are signs of age and injury, chips in horn tips, faint cracks in older scales, and a general roughness that speaks to years of wear
His eyes burn almost as sharp as his breath is hot, pale blue eyes, like frost catching the glow of a forge, they are sharp, watchful and intelligence, constantly watching incase a fight breaks out across the bar.
He dresses for practicality, a thick worn shirt, sleeves rolled up to accomodate his scaled arms, and a heavy leather apron darkened by years if spills, soot and heat, the apron itself bears a faint empossing of a clawed symbol, warn nearly smooth from time.

## Background
Uriel was not always a tavern keeper, nor a man content to stand behind a bar and listen to the troubles of others. There was a time when his name carried weight far beyond the walls of [[Eldermount]], spoken in guild halls, recorded in careful script, and remembered by those who survived the results of his work. In his earlier years, he was known as one of the foremost alchemists of his age, a pioneer of Draconic Alchemy, working with substances most would never dare approach, condensed elemental essence, crystallized arcane residue, and blood drawn from creatures far older than himself. His creations blurred the line between enhancement and ruin, and for a time, kings, warbands, and adventurers alike sought him out.

It was during this time, roughly a century or more ago, that Uriel crossed paths with [[Cael Varen]]. They were already old then, distant and difficult to read, but precise in both craft and conflict. The two found themselves travelling alongside a small band, bound more by circumstance than shared purpose. Where Uriel pushed limits, driven by curiosity and the pursuit of something greater, [[Cael Varen]]remained measured, controlled, and unchanging, a quiet counterbalance to Uriel’s ambition. It is said that some of Uriel’s finest work was shaped in those years, tempered not just by his own hand, but by the presence of one who understood the weight of creation in a way few others could.

But something changed. Whether it was a failed experiment, a realization, or something that happened during those years on the road, Uriel does not say. What is known is that he walked away. He abandoned his laboratories, his research, and the reputation he had built, eventually resurfacing in [[Eldermount]] as the owner of the [[Dragonclaw Tavern]]. He no longer practices alchemy, and with the leadership of [[Eldermount's Alchemist Guild]] long since changed, his name has faded from memory. Those who know him now see only a quiet tavern keeper, though on rare occasions, when the forge burns late and the past feels closer than it should, it is said he still speaks with  [[Cael Varen]] in a way that suggests neither of them ever truly left those years behind.
